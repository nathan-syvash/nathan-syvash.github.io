2025 Internship at Ithaca Clean Energy

Built IoT-enabled environmental monitoring systems for industrial fishing and seafood storage, with a focus on a secure, local-first LoRaWAN infrastructure that could scale without relying on third-party data platforms.

Company
Ithaca Clean Energy
Focus
LoRaWAN, Raspberry Pi, ChirpStack, CAD, GD&T, product development
Outcome
Delivered a functioning local-first monitoring stack and supplier-ready design documentation for hardware handoff and scaling.

During my internship at Ithaca Clean Energy, I worked on developing IoT-enabled monitoring systems for industrial fishing and seafood storage. My focus was designing and deploying a LoRaWAN-based infrastructure that ensured secure, reliable, and scalable environmental monitoring without relying on external third-party systems.

IoT Infrastructure for Environmental Monitoring

I designed and implemented a LoRaWAN sensor network to automate refrigeration monitoring and environmental compliance in seafood storage units. The system was built to be secure, reliable, and easy to extend, while also addressing vendor concerns about data ownership and third-party access.

  • Selected and configured a Milesight UG63 gateway and EM320 temperature sensors to establish the core hardware network.
  • Deployed a Raspberry Pi as a local server, hardcoded ChirpStack, and manually integrated sensor-to-server connections.
  • Built a simple real-time data logging dashboard to support visualization, compliance tracking, and alerts.
  • Designed the system with end-to-end encryption and local-first data ownership for privacy and security.

This infrastructure let storage facility managers keep their data locally, reduced manual logging from 4–5 checks per day to automated monitoring, and supported future scaling to additional chemical, environmental, and mechanical sensors.

Ultrasonic Sensor Mounting CAD

Alongside the digital infrastructure, I also designed mounting hardware for the NMEA 2000 Ultrasonic TLM100 Sensor. The goal was to make the part vendor-ready, structurally stiff, and straightforward to assemble in practice.

  • Produced fully annotated 2D CAD drawing packages with GD&T tolerances, vendor-ready title blocks, and BOMs.
  • Added bosses, alignment features, and wiring clearances to support stable mounting and assembly ease.
  • Designed for structural stiffness while minimizing material usage.

Research and Reports

Beyond implementation, I researched sensor-driven monitoring for industrial fishing and prepared reports on storage-unit IoT architectures. That work compared communication technologies including WiFi, Bluetooth, and LoRa, and supported the recommendation to use LoRaWAN as the most robust solution for the application. These reports helped guide design choices and evaluate how the system could scale across broader seafood logistics use cases.

Key Takeaways

  • Built a full IoT infrastructure from concept to deployment.
  • Translated client needs such as privacy, reliability, and scalability into engineering requirements.
  • Delivered both digital systems, including the data pipeline and dashboard, and physical solutions through CAD sensor mounts and documentation.

By the end of the internship, I delivered a fully functioning LoRaWAN infrastructure and design documentation that could be handed off for external manufacturing and scaled across multiple sites.
