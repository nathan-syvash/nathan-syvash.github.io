2025 — Ithaca Clean Energy

Monitoring Systems Engineering

Designed and deployed monitoring infrastructure across two applications: a local-first IoT sensor network for industrial seafood storage, and a coastal vessel-tracking system using LiDAR and camera hardware for the City.

Company
Ithaca Clean Energy
Focus
LoRaWAN, LiDAR, sensor selection, hardware trade studies, RFQs, CAD, GD&T
Outcomes
Deployed LoRaWAN stack from concept to handoff. Delivered a sensor architecture recommendation and installation requirements for a $50K–$60K coastal monitoring system.
Project 1 — Summer 2025

IoT Infrastructure for Environmental Monitoring

I designed and deployed a LoRaWAN sensor network to automate refrigeration monitoring and environmental compliance for industrial seafood storage. The system needed to be secure, reliable, and extensible, while keeping all data local to address client concerns about third-party access.

  • Selected and configured a Milesight UG63 gateway and EM320 temperature sensors to establish the core hardware network.
  • Deployed a Raspberry Pi as a local server, configured ChirpStack, and manually integrated sensor-to-server connections.
  • Built a real-time data logging dashboard to support visualization, compliance tracking, and alerts.
  • Designed the system with end-to-end encryption and local-first data ownership for privacy and security.

The deployed system reduced manual logging from 4–5 checks per day to automated monitoring and was architected to scale to additional chemical, environmental, and mechanical sensors across multiple sites.

Ultrasonic Sensor Mounting CAD

Alongside the digital infrastructure, I designed mounting hardware for the NMEA 2000 Ultrasonic TLM100 Sensor. The goal was a vendor-ready part that was structurally stiff and practical to assemble in the field.

  • Produced fully annotated 2D drawings with GD&T tolerances, vendor-ready title blocks, and BOMs.
  • Added bosses, alignment features, and wiring clearances to support stable mounting and assembly ease.
  • Designed for structural stiffness while minimizing material usage.

Communication Technology Research

I prepared reports comparing communication technologies including WiFi, Bluetooth, and LoRa for industrial fishing and seafood storage applications. This analysis supported the recommendation to use LoRaWAN as the most reliable and scalable solution, and helped guide architecture decisions as the system expanded to additional use cases.

Project 2 — Jan. 2026 – June 2026

Coastal Vessel Monitoring in the Bay Area

I led a hardware trade study to design a deployable vessel-monitoring system for an estuary in the Bay Area. The system needed to track vessels at 200–400 m range in a marine environment, integrating LiDAR and camera hardware while staying within a defined budget and installation footprint.

LiDAR Hardware Selection

Evaluating LiDAR platforms for a coastal marine environment required more than comparing specs. Water introduces specific optical challenges: certain wavelengths are heavily absorbed by water and create noise in returns, which directly affects detection reliability at range. I used this to narrow the platform shortlist before scoring other criteria.

  • Evaluated 5 LiDAR platforms against range, wavelength, IP rating, integration complexity, and cost.
  • Identified wavelength as a primary filter: selected platforms operating at wavelengths with low water absorption to minimize surface noise and false returns in the estuary environment.
  • Scored each platform across coverage, environmental rating, integration risk, and procurement lead time to produce a ranked recommendation.

Camera Selection and Coverage Analysis

I evaluated both fixed and PTZ camera configurations for the 200–400 m tracking range, weighing field of view, resolution at distance, and the tradeoffs between static coverage and active tracking.

  • Compared fixed and PTZ options across field of view, resolution at range, power requirements, and mounting flexibility.
  • Assessed integration risk for each configuration, including pan/tilt control overhead and latency considerations for vessel tracking.
  • Combined camera and LiDAR coverage maps to validate that the proposed layout met the 200–400 m detection requirement.

Supplier Communication and RFQs

Hardware selection fed directly into supplier outreach. I prepared RFQ packages and communicated with vendors and installers to validate lead times, confirm compatibility, and surface procurement risks before the architecture was finalized.

  • Built RFQ packages for shortlisted LiDAR and camera hardware, including specification summaries and environmental requirements for vendor review.
  • Communicated with suppliers and installers to confirm mounting options, IP ratings in marine exposure, and delivery timelines.
  • Used supplier feedback to refine the final architecture and flag substitution risks ahead of procurement.

Physical Constraints and Installation Requirements

Deploying hardware in a coastal marine environment introduced constraints that had to be defined before installation could be scoped. I authored a set of installation requirements covering the physical and environmental conditions the system would face.

  • Defined mounting requirements based on site geometry, sensor field-of-view angles, and structural load considerations.
  • Specified corrosion protection, vibration tolerance, and ingress protection standards for hardware exposed to salt air and wave motion.
  • Documented power supply, cable routing, and commissioning procedures to support installation by a third-party team.

Architecture Recommendation

The trade study resulted in a recommended field-deployable monitoring architecture in the $50K–$60K range for the City. The recommendation covered sensor configuration, system layout, procurement risk, and a path to installation — giving the client a complete basis for moving into procurement and deployment.
