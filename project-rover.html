Remote Complex Terrain Rover

Designed and fabricated a remote-controlled rover capable of navigating complex terrain, collecting objects, and scaling a vertical cliff, with a focus on wheel design, structural validation, and terrain adaptability.

Focus
SolidWorks, FEA, DFMEA-style validation, 3D printing, rover design
Responsibilities
Wheel design, weight distribution, structural validation
Outcome
Delivered a robust rover that completed the course in 5:32, collected all objects, and climbed the cliff.

Objective: Design and fabricate a remote-controlled rover capable of navigating complex terrain, collecting objects, and scaling a vertical cliff.

  • Achieved CSWA certification, demonstrating proficiency in mechanical CAD, assemblies, and GD&T-based drawings.
  • Built experience with additive fabrication, including Prusa and Ultimaker 3D printing, and subtractive fabrication including CNC laser cutting and milling.
  • Applied motor characterization to connect drive performance with mechanical design decisions.
  • Performed FEA to validate gearbox alignment, chassis stiffness, and gripper durability.

Personal Contribution

  • Designed and fabricated large front wheels and a back caster wheel with 3D-printed PLA cores and TPU tires for improved durability and traction.
  • Ran finite element analyses on custom wheel iterations to validate stiffness, stress distribution, and load capacity.
  • Improved wheel geometry through DFMEA-style validation, reducing deflection by 77% and peak stress by 76% between Iteration 2 and Iteration 3.
  • Selected the Spiral Quarterly Flex wheel as the final design because of its uniform stress distribution, low weight, and efficient 4.8-hour print time.
  • Improved terrain adaptability by increasing the wheel’s ability to deform into uneven surfaces.
  • Optimized the rover’s center of gravity to improve incline stability and obstacle traversal.
  • Designed iterations of the gripper and climbing mechanism using CAD tools.

Wheel Validation

Objective: Engineer a set of modular rover wheels capable of supporting structural loads, gripping uneven terrain, and withstanding impacts while remaining lightweight and manufacturable via additive processes.

  • Material properties: PLA (E = 30.5 ksi, ν = 0.28, σᵧ = 32 psi) parameterized in simulation.
  • Boundary conditions: 10 N static shaft load applied on the metal hub, with contact constrained to the ground plane.
  • Analysis metrics: von Mises stress, displacement plots, and DFMEA-style risk analysis for buckling, excessive deflection, and uneven stress.

Iterative Design Outcomes

Iteration 1 - V-Flex

TPU tire plus PLA hub. This version showed excessive deformation, with 6.3 cm deflection under 0.5 kg and about 64% compression during experimental testing, so it was deprioritized.

Iteration 2 - Triangular Flex

PLA wheel with radiating triangular walls. It achieved 0.61 mm deflection and 3.9 MPa maximum stress. It supported the load, but the stress distribution remained uneven.

Iteration 3 - Spiral Quarterly Flex

PLA wheel with spikelike straight supports. This version reduced deflection to 0.14 mm and peak stress to 0.92 MPa, with much more uniform stress distribution. It was selected as the final design because of its stiffness, low weight, and fast print time.

Wheel Validation Report

Iteration 4 - Final

After on-site testing, the core of Iteration 3 was changed to PLA while the outside ring remained TPU. This combination performed better while climbing the cliff and became the final field-proven version.

Outcome

A robust, modular, and highly functional rover that showcased iterative design, mechanical analysis, and practical problem-solving for demanding terrain traversal and object collection tasks.

Performance Highlights

  • Completed the course in 5:32, successfully collecting all objects and climbing the cliff.
  • Gearbox and winch system delivered precise climbing capability.
  • Gripper handled objects efficiently with minimal deformation-related issues.
  • Rover maintained strong structural integrity despite drops and rough terrain.
