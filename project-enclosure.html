Dentist Office Recording Device

Developed a robust, manufacturable enclosure for a dental-office recording device designed to distinguish machine sounds from human speech, supporting HIPAA and FDA compliance while improving clinic scheduling efficiency.

Role
Mechanical Design Lead (Enclosure)
Focus
SolidWorks, GD&T, enclosure design, DVP&R-style validation, IP44 features
Outcome
Delivered a validated, vendor-ready enclosure and a working device prototype handed off for future commercialization.

Objective: Develop a robust, manufacturable device enclosure capable of withstanding handling and environmental loads while maintaining ergonomic form and HIPAA/FDA compliance.

Overview

The device leverages machine audio profiles to distinguish between dental procedure sounds and human voices. This allows compliance with HIPAA and FDA regulations while improving clinic scheduling efficiency by automatically tracking procedure durations.

My Contributions

  • Created a SolidWorks enclosure model with ergonomic features, fastener bosses, alignment details, and IP44 water-resistant features.
  • Produced fully annotated 2D drawings with GD&T tolerances, section views, and a vendor-ready title block.
  • Executed DVP&R-style validation including 3 ft drop tests, static load testing, and waterproofing validation.
  • Adjusted wall thickness and geometry to improve robustness while keeping the design manufacturable.

Validation

To ensure the enclosure could withstand operational stresses and environmental handling, I ran a structural validation workflow modeled on DVP&R and DFMEA-style methods.

  • Static Load Test: Simulated distributed loading equivalent to a person sitting on the device. von Mises stress remained well below the ABS yield strength of about 74 MPa.
  • Drop Test Simulation: Modeled a 3 ft free-fall impact and evaluated displacement fields across the casing. Deformation stayed localized and within safe tolerance levels.
  • Iterative Refinement: Adjusted rib geometry and wall thickness based on stress concentrations to reduce peak deflection and improve structural reliability.
  • Water Resistance Validation: Designed for IP44 sealing performance through enclosure geometry and gasket fit, then validated with controlled splash and exposure testing.

Outcomes

I delivered a manufacturable enclosure with full GD&T drawings and vendor-ready documentation, validated through structural and environmental testing. The final prototype demonstrated durability, robustness, and alignment with usability and regulatory requirements.

Overall Product Performance

  • Signal Performance: Captured more than 80% of machine noise within the target frequency band while attenuating human speech.
  • Privacy Assurance: Prevented reconstruction of human voice content while preserving about 90% of machine-sound integrity after filtering.
  • Reliability: Demonstrated mechanical safety and robustness through environmental and structural testing.
  • User Integration: Produced a compact, durable, and intuitive device suited for practical use in dentist office settings.

The completed device was successfully delivered to the sponsoring company, which took ownership of the design for future product development and commercialization.

Next Steps
